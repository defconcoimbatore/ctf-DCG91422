ELIGIBILITY & REGISTRATION

1.	Open Participation. This CTF is open to all skill levels - students, professionals, and enthusiasts alike. Participants of all backgrounds are welcome.
2.	One Account Per Person. Each team member may register and operate only one account. 
3.	Accurate Registration. All registration information submitted must be accurate and truthful. Falsified names, institutional affiliations, or contact details are grounds for disqualification.
4.	Organizer Ineligibility. Event organizers, challenge authors and anyone else involved in the creation of the competition are not allowed to participate.
5.	Respectful Conduct. All participants must always conduct themselves with professionalism and respect. 
6.	Collusion. Any kind of collusion between teams is strictly prohibited.
7.	No Infrastructure Attacks. Any kind of attacks on the hosting infrastructure including DDoS, brute forcing etc. is strictly prohibited 
8.	Challenge Scope. Stay within the scope of the challenge and do not modify any files that are required for the challenges (e.g., deleting files, changing passwords)
9.	Flag Format. Flags will follow the format: DEFCON{...} unless a challenge explicitly states a different format. Submissions not matching the expected format will be rejected.
10.	No Flag Sharing. Sharing flag values with any other registered participant, whether directly or through third parties, is a disqualifying offense for all parties involved.
11.	Official Channels. All official announcements, clarifications, and updates will be posted on the designated communication platform. It is the participant's responsibility to monitor these channels.
12.	Eligibility for Prizes. Only participants who have fully complied with these rules are eligible for prizes. Organizers may conduct post-competition verification before awarding prizes.
13.	Verification. Prize winners may be required to provide valid government-issued identification, proof of student status (if applicable), or a brief verbal/written explanation of how they solved selected challenges.
14.	Prize Forfeiture. Any prize winner found to have violated these rules — even after the competition has concluded — will forfeit their prize and be subject to retroactive disqualification.
15.	Organizer Authority. The organizing team holds final authority on all matters related to rules interpretation, enforcement, and dispute resolution. Their decisions are binding and non-negotiable.
16.	Ban Duration. Bans issued for serious violations (e.g., infrastructure attacks, flag sharing, fraud) may be permanent and extend to all future events organized by NerdLabs Lab and DEFCON.
17.	Applicable Law. Participation in this CTF does not grant any license to perform security testing against systems outside of designated challenge environments. All activities outside the CTF scope remain subject to applicable cybersecurity and computer fraud laws.
18.	Data Privacy. Registration data collected by the organizers will be used solely for event administration purposes and will not be sold or shared with third parties, except as required by law.
19.	Recording & Streaming. The organizers reserve the right to record, stream, or broadcast competition activity and participant usernames. Participants consent to this upon registration.
20.	Liability Waiver. DEFCON Coimbatore, nerdsLab or OWASP SREC are not liable for any damages, losses, or legal consequences arising from a participant's actions during or after the CTF.
21.	Rule Amendments. The organizers reserve the right to amend, add, or remove rules at any time. Material changes will be announced through official channels and take effect immediately upon announcement.
